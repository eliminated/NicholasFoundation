Overview

The Nicholas Foundation ("TNF", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data with full transparency. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding that information when you use our websites, APIs, and open-source platforms.

We operate on a privacy-by-design principle: we collect the minimum data necessary, store it securely, and never sell it to third parties.

Data We Collect

We collect data in two categories:

  • Data you provide directly: Name, email, organization, and message content when contacting us, applying for roles, or subscribing to updates.
  • Data collected automatically: Anonymized usage statistics from our APIs (request counts, error rates, geographic region no PII), browser type, and referrer URLs for our website analytics.

We do not collect: individual user behavioral tracking, advertising identifiers, device fingerprints, or any data that would identify individual users without explicit consent.

How We Use Your Data

We use collected data exclusively to:

  • Respond to inquiries, partnership requests, and job applications
  • Send the newsletter and research updates you subscribed to (you may unsubscribe at any time)
  • Monitor API health, detect abuse, and improve platform reliability
  • Understand aggregate usage patterns to prioritize development efforts
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service

We never use your data for advertising, profiling, or commercial purposes unrelated to our mission.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: Trusted vendors who operate our email infrastructure and hosting bound by strict data processing agreements.
  • Legal compliance: If required by law, court order, or to protect the rights and safety of users.
  • With your consent: For example, if you opt in to a partnership directory or public contributor listing.

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction: Update inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Deletion: Request erasure of your personal data
  • Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection: Object to certain processing activities
  • Withdrawal: Withdraw consent at any time (e.g., newsletter unsubscribe)

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@nicholasfoundation.org. We respond to all requests within 30 days.

Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses minimal, privacy-respecting analytics. We use first-party cookies only no third-party advertising cookies, no cross-site tracking. The cookies we set include:

  • tnf-theme stores your dark/light mode preference (session functionality)
  • Session cookies for contact form CSRF protection
  • Analytics cookies (anonymized, with IP masking enabled)

You can disable cookies in your browser settings. Essential functionality cookies cannot be disabled without affecting site operation.

Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
  • AES-256 encryption for sensitive data at rest
  • Regular third-party security audits (reports published publicly)
  • Responsible disclosure program for vulnerability reporting
  • SOC 2 Type II compliance for enterprise data handling

To report a security vulnerability, please email security@nicholasfoundation.org with details. We aim to acknowledge all reports within 48 hours.

Terms of Service

By using TNF websites, APIs, or platforms, you agree to these Terms. If you do not agree, please refrain from using our services.

TNF grants you a worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use our open-source software in accordance with each project's stated license (typically MIT). This website and its content are subject to additional terms below.

Acceptable Use Policy

You must not use TNF platforms to:

  • Engage in scraping, spidering, or crawling at rates that harm platform availability
  • Violate the rights of other users or third parties
  • Develop weapons, surveillance systems, or systems designed to harm individuals
  • Circumvent API rate limits through technical means
  • Misrepresent your identity or affiliation when accessing partner-tier services

Violations may result in access revocation. Serious violations will be reported to appropriate authorities.

Intellectual Property

TNF software is open-source and licensed under MIT (or as specified per project). Research publications are typically released under Creative Commons CC BY 4.0 attribution required.

Contact & Governance

For privacy inquiries: privacy@nicholasfoundation.org
For legal matters: legal@nicholasfoundation.org
For security reports: security@nicholasfoundation.org

The Nicholas Foundation is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization. Our governing board oversees all data governance policies and conducts annual privacy reviews. Board meeting minutes and financial reports are published annually on our website.
